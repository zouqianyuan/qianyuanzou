I am a Postdoctoral Researcher at the Immersive Design Group at Southern University of Science and Technology. I recently completed my PhD in Design at Empathic Computing Lab at the University of Auckland, where my research focused on asymmetric input for 3D sketching and object editing in virtual reality. Across research, design, and visual practice, I explore how immersive systems can support creative work, inclusive interaction, and future mixed reality experiences.

Research Highlights

Designing immersive interfaces that are precise, expressive, and accessible

My work explores how interaction techniques, input devices, and visual systems shape immersive creative workflows. I am especially interested in design tasks that require precision, embodiment, and human-centered evaluation.

Immersive Interaction

Exploring asymmetric input, mixed reality interfaces, and task-specific interaction techniques for 3D sketching, object editing, and design workflows in XR.

Selected Projects

Interaction for Design, Culture, and Accessibility

A selection of research and design work exploring how interactive systems support creative practice, cultural understanding, and accessible experiences.

Asymmetric Input for 3D Sketching in Virtual Reality

My PhD research focused on asymmetric input, investigating how different types of input devices and interaction modalities influence precision, usability, and comfort in immersive design tasks, including 3D tracing, sketching, and object editing.

VR HCI 3D interaction
View project

Immersive Documentation for Intangible Cultural Heritage

At the PolyU–NVIDIA Joint Research Centre, I conducted research and prototyping on spatial capture and immersive presentation for preserving craft knowledge. This work involved collaborating with Lin Shiyuan, a national-level inheritor of the She ethnic group’s silver forging craftsmanship, to explore how 3D and XR media can make cultural practices more legible and accessible.

XR capture Point clouds Heritage

Designing Accessible Interactive Systems

A research stream focused on accessibility, rehabilitation, and supportive everyday interaction. From VR drawing tools for people with Parkinson's to context-aware AI for ageing in place, this work investigates how technology can support expression, dignity, and independence.
